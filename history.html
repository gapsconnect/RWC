Royal Westerns Club, officially registered on September 15, 2015

Royal Westerns Club, officially registered on September 15, 2015, was founded by passionate Nepali youths in Western Melbourne. The club aims to create a community platform for football enthusiasts to manage stress and build strong social connections.

Since its inception, the club has grown into a robust organization, participating and achieving success in various tournaments like the Victoria Soccer Division One League, GNBC League 35+, Royal Cup, and several multicultural soccer tournaments.

Beyond football, the club is dedicated to social initiatives, organizing blood donation drives, COVID-19 relief programs, First aid & CPR training, and public speaking training. This commitment to community service, combined with consistent sports performance, has garnered recognition from prestigious organizations and sponsors. The club continues to host events and collaborate with other social organizations, promoting a healthy and active lifestyle.

september 15 2015, Establishment

The Royal Westerns Club was born out of the enthusiasm and dedication of seven founding members. These individuals, driven by a common love for sports and community, each contributed $100 to establish the foundation of our club. Their vision and hard work have created a thriving community space for like-minded individuals.
